This project implements a real-time robotic manipulation system for warehouse-style pick-and-place tasks using a UR7e robotic arm. The system combines vision-based perception, inverse kinematics, and a joint-space Model Predictive Controller (MPC) to execute safe, smooth, and adaptive manipulation in cluttered environments.

The work emphasizes deployable robotics: tight control loops, explicit constraints, and robustness to perception updates ‚Äî aligning closely with industrial and research robotics problems tackled at NVIDIA.

üß† Problem

  • Robotic arms must operate safely near obstacles and joint limits
  • Perception updates dynamically as objects are detected and classified
  • Classical motion planning struggles with online updates and constraints
  • Real systems require predictive, constraint-aware control

The challenge is to close the loop between perception and control without replanning everything from scratch.

This approach enables smooth motion even when targets or constraints change online ‚Äî a key requirement for intelligent manipulation systems.
